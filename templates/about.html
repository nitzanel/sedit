SeDIT tool is used for comparison of expression levels of specific genes between males' and females' 11 immune cell types.
There are 4 independent RNA-sequencing datasets. All datasets were produced according to ImmGen1 pipeline (http://www.immgen.org/Protocols/11Cells.pdf). Alignment to the genome and quantification were done by Hisat2 and Tuxedo3 pipeline.
Datasets description:
All datasets are RNA- sequencing data from male and female mice of 11 naïve immune cell types:

  1. Granulocytes (GN)
  2. Macrophages (MF)
  3. Dendritic Cells (DC)
  4. B1a B cells (B1a)
  5. B Cells (B)
  6. Natural Killer Cells (NK)
  7. CD8+ T Cells (T8)
  8. CD4+ T Cells (T4)
  9. Regulatory T Cells (Treg)
  10. Natural Killer T Cells (NKT)
  11. Gamma Delta T Cells (Tgd)



Dataset Description Mapped reads per sample
DS_A 3 replicates. Including interferon (IFN)

DS_B Samples from young (2 months), adult (6 months) and old (17 months males, 20 months females) mice.
No replicates (two replicates of 6 months B and T4 samples). 		7-18 M (mean 12)
DS_C No replicates. Male and female samples were produced in separate batches. Males: 19-117 M (mean 70)
Females: 7-41 M (mean 25)
DS_D No replicates. 4-20 M (mean 13)

SeDIT allows browsing at two levels of resolution:

Per cell type - presenting replicates of specific cell types from all datasets, figure 2.
